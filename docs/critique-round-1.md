# Demo Critique — Round 1

## Summary
- Overall score: 5.7/10
- Critical issues: 5

## Frame-by-Frame Critique

### Frame 1: Initial state (`~0:00`)
**Screenshot evidence:** Seattle map view fills the portrait frame with one numbered marker near the city, almost no route context, and the playback bar parked at the bottom.
**Issues:**
1. [CRITICAL] The opening shot has no hook. It does not establish Seattle as a chapter; it looks like a paused editor, not the first shot of a reel.
2. [MAJOR] The light basemap is so washed out that the frame reads as low-contrast fog instead of intentional whitespace.
3. [MAJOR] The playback bar is immediately in-shot, which breaks the fantasy that this is a finished video.
4. [MINOR] The lone numbered marker has no meaning to a first-time viewer.
**What's good:** The base map is clean and not visually noisy.

### Frame 2: Seattle hover / takeoff (`~0:02`)
**Screenshot evidence:** The camera jumps to a Pacific globe, a small plane sits near center, and the Seattle→Honolulu route chip appears low in frame.
**Issues:**
1. [CRITICAL] The product skips past Seattle too fast; there is no satisfying city-hover beat before the first travel leg.
2. [MAJOR] In 9:16, the globe composition makes the actual subject feel tiny and stranded in empty space.
3. [MAJOR] The plane icon looks toy-like and undersized relative to the amount of empty ocean around it.
4. [MINOR] The route chip hugs the lower third and feels mechanically placed rather than art-directed.
**What's good:** The route itself is easy to parse at a glance.

### Frame 3: Seattle → Honolulu flight (`~0:02–0:03`)
**Screenshot evidence:** Pacific globe, plane centered over the ocean, Seattle/Honolulu markers visible, route history beginning to accumulate.
**Issues:**
1. [MAJOR] The camera is still too far out; most of the frame is dead globe instead of movement.
2. [MAJOR] The travel icon does not feel premium enough for a cinematic demo.
3. [MINOR] Breadcrumb numbers are readable only as dots with digits, not as meaningful progress indicators.
**What's good:** The sense of large-scale travel is understandable.

### Frame 4: Honolulu arrival (`~0:05`)
**Screenshot evidence:** Honolulu label at the top, two oversized white photo cards floating over a gray map wash.
**Issues:**
1. [CRITICAL] The cards waste vertical space with huge white mats while the actual photos remain shallow, letterboxed strips.
2. [MAJOR] The captions are far too small to read comfortably on a phone-sized export.
3. [MAJOR] The map is reduced to a flat gray backdrop, so the arrival loses place and atmosphere.
4. [MINOR] The top label feels detached from the photo composition below it.
**What's good:** The city chip itself is legible and pleasantly styled.

### Frame 5: Honolulu photo card hold (`~0:05`)

## Prioritized Fix List
1. [CRITICAL] Rework 9:16 photo presentation again. The cards still waste height, captions are still too small, and Tokyo still collapses into too many tiny items.
2. [CRITICAL] Fix the incorrect Alishan photo asset. One obviously wrong image is enough to make the whole demo feel fake.
3. [CRITICAL] Redesign the opening and ending beats so Seattle actually gets an introduction and a payoff.
4. [MAJOR] Replace the repeated “small plane on giant globe” composition with tighter, more directed camera framing for long-haul flights in portrait mode.
5. [MAJOR] Hide or restyle playback/editor chrome for demo capture so the result looks like a finished reel, not a paused editing surface.
6. [MAJOR] Simplify breadcrumb and marker language in 9:16 so progress reads as intentional storytelling rather than leftover editor metadata.
7. [MAJOR] Increase caption size and contrast across all photo chapters.
8. [MINOR] Tune transport icon scale and visual style so they feel less like stickers.
